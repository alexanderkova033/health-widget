Open the curtains as soon as you can. Even on a grey day, outdoor light is roughly ten times brighter than a well-lit room.

A short walk before you start work can end up improving your focus for hours afterward.

Even 10 minutes of morning activity is often enough to lift your mood for the rest of the day, per research on single short workouts.
If you eat breakfast, pairing carbs with a bit of protein blunts the sugar spike, and researchers call the effect a "second-meal effect" since it can carry through to lunch.
Outdoor light is far brighter than any indoor room, even on a cloudy day, and that's still enough to help set your body clock.
Cortisol rises 60 to 150 percent in the half hour after you wake up. That's a normal part of the process, not a sign that something's wrong.

Morning exercise doesn't have to be intense. Brisk walking alone raises your heart rate enough to boost alertness.
